Biography
Short Bio (50 words)
Pablo Talavera is a multidisciplinary performance artist specialising in physical theatre, circus arts, and immersive experiences. With 8 years of professional practice across Europe and Asia, he merges movement, fire, and space design to create unforgettable live events.
Full Bio (150 words)
Pablo Talavera is a European-based performance artist whose work sits at the intersection of physical theatre, contemporary circus, and immersive experience design. His practice draws on 8 years of professional performance, spanning contact staff juggling, fire performance, flying pole, partner acrobatics, and contact improvisation.

As a stage and space designer, Pablo has built sets and festival architectures for over 20 events across Europe — from intimate wellness retreats to large-scale festival main stages. His teaching practice includes 7 specialist workshops covering physical preparation, spatial awareness, partner movement, acrobatics, and flow arts.

Committed to the idea that performance can dissolve the boundary between artist and audience, Pablo brings a poetic, precise, and occasionally absurd energy to every project he takes on.

Technical Rider
Fire Performance Requirements
  • Min. 8×8m performance area, clear of audience
  • 2× fire safety officers on standby
  • Fire blanket + CO₂ extinguisher on site
  • Dipping bucket (paraffin) provided by performer
  • Outdoor only — wind < 5 Beaufort
  • 45 min setup + 20 min safety briefing
Workshop Space Requirements
  • Min. 8×8m sprung or matted floor
  • No fixed furniture or obstructions
  • Sound system (optional but preferred)
  • Changing room access
  • Max 16 participants for acro workshops
Stage Performance
  • Min. 6×6m clear stage area
  • Side-lighting preferred
  • Sound system: stereo min, aux input required
  • 30 min pre-show access for warm-up
