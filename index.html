The Duo

Kaida & Pablo — One Movement

Kaidablo is the creative partnership of Kaida and Pablo — two artists whose complementary styles create something unrepeatable. Each show is a conversation: between bodies, between discipline and instinct, between the stillness beneath the flame and the force that feeds it.

Drawing on contact dance, martial arts, and contemporary movement, they weave these disciplines into a shared physical language that explores trust, risk, and human connection.

About
Kaidablo

"Each performance is a conversation: between bodies, between discipline and instinct, between the stillness beneath the flame and the force that feeds it."

Kaidablo is the creative partnership of Kaida and Pablo. Drawing on contact dance, martial arts, and contemporary movement, they weave these disciplines into a shared physical language — exploring trust, risk, and connection as an evolving physical conversation.

01
Kaida
Martial Arts · Fire · Aerials · Wushu

Precision meets power. Kaida brings the discipline of Wushu and Filipino Martial Arts together with fire, aerials, and contemporary movement. A warrior's body with an artist's sensitivity — sharp, intuitive, and deeply physical.

Specialising in film fighting and stage combat, Kaida creates moments of intense clarity in motion — from TKD kicks to seamless partner acrobatics. She holds the sword and the soft lift with equal mastery.

02
Pablo
Physical Theatre · Circus · Stage Design

Pablo Talavera is a multidisciplinary performance artist with around 8 years of professional experience. His work combines movement, space, and light to create emotionally engaging and interactive performances — bringing a poetic, physical, and sometimes absurd sensibility to everything he creates.

From building yurts at 20+ festivals to coordinating fire safety for 350+ visitors at Fai Jai 2025, from contact staff to flying pole — Pablo is as comfortable constructing the stage as inhabiting it. His solo work "Between Worlds / Dream Sand" tours internationally in German and English.

Philosophy

The Negative
Space

Their shows investigate how tension, proximity, and complementary movement qualities create a dialogue between two movers. The choreography plays with contrast between softness and intensity, control and surrender.

Drawing on contact dance, martial arts, and contemporary movement, they weave these disciplines into a shared physical language — incorporating fire props and shifting physical relationships to navigate moments of attraction, resistance, and balance.
